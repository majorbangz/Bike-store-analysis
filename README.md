# Bike-store-analysis
The Problem?
The bike store had data on 1,000 customers but no clear picture of who was actually buying. Nearly half weren't converting, and there was no understanding of why — whether it was age, income, location, or something else entirely.

The Purpose?
The goal was to dig into the customer data and find patterns. Who buys? Who doesn't? And what does the typical buyer actually look like? The findings could help the store focus its marketing on the right people instead of guessing.

What's in the file:

bike_buyers — raw data
Working Sheet — cleaned up, with age brackets added
Pivot Table — income by gender and purchase decision
Dashboard — interactive charts with slicers for region, education, and marital status

What I found!!

Middle-aged customers (30s–50s) had the highest conversion rate at 54.6% and made up 70% of the dataset. The Pacific region outperformed North America despite North America having nearly three times more customers. Short-commute buyers (0–5 miles) converted better than long-distance ones — bikes here are more of a lifestyle purchase than a commuter tool. Buyers also earned about $3,000 more per year on average than non-buyers.

Tools
Excel — pivot tables, slicers, bar charts, dashboard
